BCNHS HEAD HAILED AS OUTSTANDING WOMAN LEADER
March 11, 2019

Baguio City National High School (BCNHS) principal Brenda Cariño has been awarded as one of the Outstanding Women Leaders (OWLs) of Baguio City during the Kababaihan Festival, Mar. 8 at the PFVR Gymnasium.

With the theme, “We Make Change Work for Women,” the event recognized Cariño’s contributions in the field of education as part of the celebration of the National Women’s Month.

Not only is she managing the largest secondary school in the city, but she is also the head of the National Association of Public Secondary School Heads (NAPSSHI) Baguio chapter, and the secretary in the regional level.

Cariño said that she is responsible in promoting and advocating programs that would empower women for leadership. self-reliance and service to others.

“I will be ready to be part of the city’s programs and activities, to coordinate with the city government regarding programs for development, especially for the women’s sector,” she said.

Cariño also wants to tell all the other women that they could be empowered and do well in the roles they take in the society.

“Let us continue to believe in ourselves that we can be the best person in the respective roles that we are called to be — a mother, a sister, a grandmother, and a friend to all; that we should be strong in all the challenges in life and believe that God is our stronghold in all these challenges,” Carino said.

CITY HIGHER TAKES MAYORAL SEAT IN SOFAD
October 15, 2019

A Girl Scout from Baguio City National High School (BCNHS) took over the mayoral seat at City Hall on Oct. 14 for the annual Scouts Official for a Day (SOFAD).

Scout Aevrylle Malihod, a Grade 10 student in the Science Technology and Engineering program, topped the examination of candidates held last Sept. 6 and the one-on-one interview on Sept. 10.

“The exam was quite difficult and I struggled, but I did my very best to pass and secure the position,” said Malihod, who also expressed her gratitude to her comrades and Troop Leader Anne Margaret Garcia.

She added that she is looking forward to taking over the position of the mayor since it would give her opportunities to hone her leadership skills.

“I’m really just happy because I will be able to experience how things work in politics, it is also good exposure anyway. I want to better my leadership skills and this will be a great step towards it, I will also be able to open my eyes to the real struggles in the world,” Malihod said.

Having secured a position in City Hall before, Malihod said she already has plans on regulations that she would like to be implemented.

“I would include strict implementation of proper waste management, incentive for honor students, and strict implementation of the anti-distraction law to the things I would like to work on in the future.”

PNP LAUNCHES INFORMATION DRIVE IN CITY HIGH
September 30, 2019

To inform the students on the different ordinances in Baguio City, the Philippine National Police conducted an information drive at Baguio City National High School (BCNHS) from June to September.

Police personnel went from room to room to orient students from grades 7 to 9 about curfews, anti-distracted walking and anti-profanity ordinances, and gang affiliation; whereas Grade 10 students were informed about anti-terrorism and illegal drug abuse.

According to Police Corporal Greggy William Biado Jr., they are conducting this information drive because of the evident lack of knowledge of the students about the said ordinances.

“We did this orientation because we observed that there is insufficient information dissemination through Faceebook and social media,” Biado said.

CITY HIGH GRAD TOPS 2019 LIBRARIAN BOARD EXAM
October 2, 2019

An alumnus of Baguio City National High School topped the recent Librarian Licensure Examination held on Sept. 10-11.

Daniel Balbin Jr., who graduated as class valedictorian of batch 2015, achieved a rating of 89.75 percent to rank first among 1,024 examinees.

As a student in the star section and writer for The Pine Tree, Balbin said he was grateful to City High for being a part of his formative years in education.

“City High is a partner. It was able to let me test a lot of many waters in order for me to cipher which water should I swim deeper or which ones should I just dip my toe,” said the magna cum laude graduate of Benguet State University.

As an achiever, Balbin gave tips on study habits that helped him succeed in his studies.

“We all have our own styles of learning and reviewing. So my word is that you review or learn with the way you are comfortable with. But if you don’t know what is comfortable for you, my tip is you should know yourself better!”

BCNHS ATHLETES SHINE IN 2019 BATANG PINOY
September 2, 2019

Baguio City National High School (BCNHS) athletes pitched in a number of medals to ensure that Baguio defends its title in this year’s Batang Pinoy finals, Aug. 25-31 in Puerto Princesa, Palawan.

In the official and final tally of the Philippine Sports Commission (PSC), Baguio led the medal haul with 61 gold, 45 silver and 35 bronze, a remarkable gap from second placer Cebu City with 36-34-35 medals.

City High athletes contributed medals in the different events they participated, with one bronze in swimming courtesy of Maenard Rhoman, a silver from Maxine Alfonso in high jump, and bronze for the futsal team.

Christian Paul and John Nicolas Paderon also emerged victorious in badminton after nailing a spot in the top three.

BRIONES VISITS CITY HIGH AFTER PISA FEAT
January 31, 2020

Department of Education Secretary Leonor Magtolis-Briones visited Baguio City National High School to personally commend the school for being the country’s top scorer in the reading comprehension results of the 2018 Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA).

“I have not come here to teach, but to learn about the teaching practices of the Baguio City National High School,” Briones said during the school’s welcome program for the secretary on January 31.

Prior to her visit to the school, Briones attended the launching of DepEd CAR’s Project READ to LEAD in Wangal, La Trinidad where Mrs. Brenda M. Cariño, Secondary School Principal IV, received the certificate of recognition from Regional Director May B. Eclar and Assistant Regional Director Florante E. Vergara.

CITY HIGH IS BRIGADA ESKWELA HALL OF FAME AWARDEE
November 29, 2019

For three consecutive years of bagging the Brigada Eskwela Best Implementer award, Baguio City National High School (BCNHS) has attained a Hall of Fame status in the Cordillera region.

Besting five big schools in the region, City High once again topped the Mega school category with Abra High School coming in second and Tublay School of Home Industries, third.

Brenda Cariño, the school principal, said that the school owes the recognition to the stakeholders and institutions who participate during the conduct of Brigada Eskwela.

“We have strengthened our partnership with our stakeholders and, of course, we acknowledge the participation of the teachers and students. Every year, they commit themselves by giving donations and assistance,” she said. Having achieved a Hall of Fame status, the school will no longer be part of the annual search for Best Brigada Implementer.

Cariño said, however, that City High still needs to sustain its implementation of Brigada projects, especially since DepEd officials will continue to evaluate and validate the school’s programs.

“We cannot be part of the competition anymore, but maybe there’s a criteria for the Most Sustained School implementer of the Brigada Eskwela. Maybe DepEd personnel will come to visit and validate what’s going on with our Brigada program,” she said.
